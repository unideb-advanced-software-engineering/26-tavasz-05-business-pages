# ADR 004: Domain Management and Collision Policy

## Context
The Zamunda Digital Renaissance (ZDR) program aims to provide an online presence for nearly one million local businesses. A core feature of the system is providing a unique identity for each entity within the national digital ecosystem. The system must handle:
* Registration of custom `.zamunda` domain names.
* Management of approximately one million potential unique web addresses.
* High heterogeneity in the digital maturity of business owners.

## Facing the Need for
* **Fair Resource Allocation:** Every registered business is entitled to exactly one free `.zamunda` domain name.
* **Collision Prevention:** The system must prevent multiple entities from claiming the same domain name or a single entity from hoarding multiple free domains.
* **Flexibility vs. Availability:** Businesses must be allowed to change their chosen domain name, but the previously used name should not remain locked indefinitely.
* **Automated Integration:** Domain management must interface with the central Zamunda DNS management system.

## We Decided for
### 1. Mandatory Identity-Linked Registration
Domain registration is strictly tied to a validated **Zamunda One** business identity. The system performs an automated check against the central tax/business registry during the domain request process to enforce the "one business, one domain" rule.

### 2. Immediate Availability Policy
In accordance with **BR-02**, when a business changes its domain name, the system will implement an "Immediate Release" mechanism. The previous domain name becomes available for registration by other entities immediately (or after a short technical grace period) to maximize the pool of available names.

### 3. Automated DNS Interface
The platform will utilize a dedicated **DNS Interface** to communicate with the central national DNS handler. This ensures that once a domain is claimed or changed in the User Administration Portal, the records are updated programmatically without manual intervention.

### 4. Suspension on Dissolution
Following **BR-04**, if a business's registration in the Zamunda One system is terminated or the business ceases to exist, its associated `.zamunda` domain will be automatically suspended and eventually returned to the public pool.

## Neglecting
* **First-Come, First-Served without Validation:** We rejected allowing domain registration without Zamunda One validation to prevent "domain squatting" by non-business entities.
* **Premium/Paid Domain Tiers:** Since the program is a state-funded initiative for digital development, all domains remain free of charge within the defined quotas.
* **Permanent Reservation:** We rejected the idea of keeping old domain names reserved for businesses after they have changed them, as this would artificially deplete the available namespace.

## Achieving
* **Equitable Access:** Every legitimate business gets an equal opportunity to have a professional web address.
* **Namespace Efficiency:** The immediate release policy ensures that high-value names are not wasted on inactive profiles.
* **System Integrity:** The link to Zamunda One guarantees that only officially registered businesses can utilize the .zamunda space.

## Accepting That (We Will Have)
* **No "Brand Protection" for Abandoned Names:** Businesses changing their names must accept that their old domain can be immediately claimed by a competitor.
* **DNS Propagation Delays:** While the system updates records immediately, global DNS propagation may still result in a transition period where the site is unreachable.
* **Dependency on National DNS:** Any outage in the state's central DNS infrastructure will directly impact the availability of all business pages.
